**Whoop:** Check recovery each morning; if in red, make the day easier (easy run or rest instead of intervals/leg day).

## Daily basics (every day)

| Item | Target | Notes |
|------|--------|------|
| **Water** | 2.0–2.5 L (8–10 cups) | More on hot days or long runs. Sip through the day. |
| **Minimum steps** | 8,000 (base) / 10,000 (target) | On top of planned runs; walking counts. |
| **Sleep** | 7–9 h, consistent wake time | Non-negotiable for fat loss and recovery. |
| **Supplements (suggested)** | See below | Adjust with your doctor (PCOS, cholesterol). |

### Recommended supplements (minimum)

- **Vitamin D3:** 1,000–2,000 IU with fat-containing meal (Patrick/Attia — many are deficient).
- **Omega-3 (EPA/DHA):** 1–2 g total daily (fish or algae oil) — lipids, inflammation (Patrick).
- **Magnesium:** 200–400 mg (e.g. glycinate) in evening — sleep, recovery (Huberman).
- **Optional:** Multivitamin or B-complex if diet is limited; iron only if bloods show need (running + meat often enough).

## Daily 5–10 min: Running & speed routine (slim legs, better form)

Do this **every day** — before a run (as part of warm-up), before gym, or as a standalone. Focus: mobility, activation, and running-specific movement to support **speed and economy** without adding bulk. Keeps legs long and responsive.

| Exercise | Duration / reps | Notes |
|----------|-----------------|--------|
| **Leg swings (forward)** | 10 each leg | Stand on one leg; swing other forward/back. Relaxed; open hip. |
| **Leg swings (side)** | 10 each leg | Swing leg across body side to side. Smooth. |
| **Ankle circles** | 8 each direction, each foot | Improves ankle stiffness and push-off. |
| **High knees** | 20–30 s | Quick, light; drive knee up, land softly. |
| **Butt kicks** | 20–30 s | Heel to glute; short, quick steps. |
| **A-skips** (or light skipping) | 20–30 s | Skip with high knee drive; rhythm and turnover. |
| **Calf raises** (bodyweight, both legs) | 15–20 reps, controlled | Full range; builds ankle stiffness for speed, not size. |
| **Quick feet** (in place or tiny steps) | 20–30 s | Fast, light feet; stay on balls of feet. |

**Order:** Swings → ankles → high knees → butt kicks → A-skips → calf raises → quick feet. **Total:** ~5–7 min. If you have 10 min, do each 30–45 s or add a second round of high knees + butt kicks + quick feet.

**Why this supports your goal:** Running economy and speed improve with mobility and neural drive (turnover, stiffness). These are low-load, high-rep or short-duration — they don’t build leg mass but do support lean, fast legs and better form.

## Morning routine (5–15 min)

- **Wake:** Same time ±30 min; light/exposure first (Huberman).
- **Water:** 1 large glass (300–400 ml) before coffee/tea.
- **Movement:** The 5–10 min running routine above, or 2–5 min stretch/walk; or do the routine before your run.
- **Optional:** 5 min breathing or calm before phone scroll.

---

## Evening routine (10–20 min)

- **Wind down:** Dim lights 1–2 h before bed; no heavy food 2–3 h before sleep.
- **Water:** Last big sip 1–2 h before bed to avoid waking.
- **Optional:** 5–10 min stretch or gentle yoga, no screens in bed.

## Gym sessions — detailed plan

**General:** Rest 60–90 s between sets. Tempo = eccentric–pause–concentric–pause (e.g. 2-1-2-0 = 2 s down, 1 s pause, 2 s up, no pause). **Heart rate:** during working sets you’ll often be in Whoop “strain” zone (roughly 70–85% max HR); not a strict target — effort and form first.

---

### Gym A — Lower + core (e.g. Monday)

| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Tempo | Rest | Notes |
|----------|------------|--------|-----|------|
| **Goblet squat** or **back squat** | 3 × 10–12 | 2-1-2-0 | 90 s | Full depth; weight so last 2 reps are hard. |
| **Romanian deadlift (RDL)** | 3 × 10–12 | 3-1-2-0 | 90 s | Feel hamstrings; slight knee bend. |
| **Walking lunges** | 2 × 12 each leg | 2-0-2-0 | 60 s | Controlled; optional dumbbells. |
| **Calf raises** (standing) | 3 × 15–20 | 2-1-2-0 | 45 s | Full range; pause at top. |
| **Plank** | 2 × 45–60 s | — | 60 s | Straight line; breathe. |
| **Dead bug** | 2 × 10 each side | Slow, controlled | 45 s | Lower back pressed to floor. |

**Duration:** ~45–55 min. **Intensity:** Moderate–hard; last set of squats/RDLs should feel challenging.

---

### Gym B — Upper + core (e.g. Wednesday)

| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Tempo | Rest | Notes |
|----------|------------|--------|-----|------|
| **Dumbbell or cable row** | 3 × 10–12 | 2-1-2-0 | 75 s | Squeeze shoulder blade. |
| **Push-ups** (or incline if needed) | 3 × 8–12 | 2-1-2-0 | 60 s | Full range; core tight. |
| **Overhead press** (DB or barbell) | 3 × 8–10 | 2-1-2-0 | 75 s | Don’t flare ribs. |
| **Lat pull-down** or **assisted pull-up** | 2 × 10–12 | 2-1-2-0 | 60 s | Control the negative. |
| **Biceps** (curl) + **triceps** (push-down or extension) | 2 × 12 each | 2-0-2-0 | 45 s | Superset to save time. |
| **Hollow hold** or **bicycle crunches** | 2 × 30 s / 2 × 15 each | — | 45 s | Core braced. |

**Duration:** ~45–55 min. **Intensity:** Moderate–hard; aim to add weight or reps over weeks.

---

### Gym C — Full body / second lower (e.g. Friday)

| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Tempo | Rest | Notes |
|----------|------------|--------|-----|------|
| **Squat** (same as Gym A) | 3 × 10 | 2-1-2-0 | 90 s | Slightly lighter than Gym A or same. |
| **RDL** | 2 × 12 | 3-1-2-0 | 90 s | Same as Gym A. |
| **Step-ups** (bench or box) | 2 × 10 each leg | 2-0-2-0 | 60 s | Drive through heel. |
| **Overhead press** | 2 × 10 | 2-1-2-0 | 75 s | Same as Gym B. |
| **Row** | 2 × 12 | 2-1-2-0 | 60 s | Same as Gym B. |
| **Plank** + **side plank** | 1 × 45 s + 1 × 30 s each side | — | 45 s | Hold good form. |

**Duration:** ~40–50 min. **Intensity:** Moderate; this is a “maintenance” full-body day.

## Running sessions — detailed plan

**Full week (earliest day first):** See **RUNNING_PLAN_WEEK.md** for the full running week (Mon → Sun) with session details, warm-up/cool-down, and why this order.

**Pace terms:**  
- **Easy:** You can speak in full sentences; nose-breathing possible. Whoop often green/low strain.  
- **Tempo:** “Comfortably hard”; short sentences only. Whoop mid–high strain.  
- **Hard/interval:** Can’t hold a conversation; a few words. Whoop high strain.

**Heart rate (rough):** Easy 65–75% max HR; tempo 80–88%; intervals 88–95%. Use Whoop zones if you have them.

---

### Long easy run (e.g. Saturday)

- **Duration:** 60–75 min.  
- **Pace:** Easy — conversational, nose-breathing possible.  
- **HR:** Stay in low–mid zone (e.g. 65–75% max HR).  
- **Route:** Flat to rolling; outside.  
- **Purpose:** Base, fat oxidation, mental. No surges; keep it even.

---

### Tempo run (e.g. Tuesday)

- **Warm-up:** 10 min easy jog.  
- **Main:** 20–25 min at tempo (comfortably hard; short sentences).  
- **Cool-down:** 8–10 min easy.  
- **Total:** ~40 min.  
- **HR:** Mid–high (e.g. 80–88% max).  
- **Purpose:** Lactate threshold, “strong” feel.

---

### Intervals (e.g. Thursday)

- **Warm-up:** 10 min easy + 4 strides.  
- **Main:** 8 × 1 min hard / 1 min easy (or 6–8 × 400 m hard with 90 s walk/jog).  
- **Cool-down:** 8–10 min easy.  
- **Total:** ~35–40 min.  
- **Hard reps:** Can’t chat; HR high (88–95% max). **Easy:** jog or walk.  
- **Purpose:** Power, leg tone, metabolism.

---

### Hills (e.g. Sunday or swap with easy)

- **Warm-up:** 10 min easy.  
- **Main:** 8–10 × 60–90 s uphill at hard effort; jog or walk down to recover.  
- **Cool-down:** 8–10 min easy.  
- **Total:** ~40–50 min.  
- **Uphill:** Strong push; HR high. **Down:** recovery only.  
- **Purpose:** Glutes, quads, power.

---

### Easy/short run (e.g. Monday or Wednesday)

- **Duration:** 25–35 min.  
- **Pace:** Easy only; very relaxed.  
- **HR:** Low (65–72% max).  
- **Purpose:** Recovery, steps, habit. Can swap for extra yoga or rest if Whoop recovery is low.

---

## Yoga

- **When:** 1–2× per week (e.g. rest days or after easy run).  
- **Format:** Follow your usual video (e.g. 30–45 min).  
- **Purpose:** Mobility, recovery, stress. No need to log details here.

## Speed things up (slightly)

- **Steps:** Aim 10,000–12,000 on non-long-run days.  
- **One extra easy movement:** 15 min walk after dinner or 10 min stretch.  
- **Keep deficit moderate:** Don’t cut calories further; prioritise protein and sleep so you don’t lose muscle or burn out.
