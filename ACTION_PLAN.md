**Goal:** Very lean, shredded look — defined abs, slim toned legs & arms (Stesha Malikova reference)

### Visual reference (dream body)
- **Abs:** Very defined six-pack, clear obliques, flat stomach, V-line toward hips.
- **Arms:** Slim, toned, visible muscle separation (shoulders, biceps); can show some vascularity when very lean.
- **Legs:** Slim, long lines, visible quad/hamstring definition without bulk.
- **Overall:** Very low body fat, strong core (e.g. plank-level strength), athletic and “shredded” not bulky.

---

## Honest reality check: 55 → 49 kg and “shredded”

**The tension:** Stesha’s look is **low body fat + visible muscle** — abs, shoulders, arms, legs with clear lines. To get there at 49 kg you need to **lose fat, not muscle**. If you drop 6 kg too fast or on low protein and little strength work, you’ll get smaller but **flat** (less definition), and you’ll feel weaker. That’s the opposite of “very strong.”

**Better approach:** Lose slowly (about **0.25–0.5 kg per week** max) with **high protein** and **3–4 strength sessions/week**. That way most of the 6 kg is fat, and you keep (or build a bit of) muscle so you actually look shredded at 49 kg. Timeline: **roughly 4–6 months** to 49 kg if you’re consistent — and the look will improve month by month, not only when you hit the number.

**49 kg at 160 cm** = BMI ~19.1 (lower end of normal). Fine if you feel good and your cycle stays regular; if either slips, pause the cut and hold at a slightly higher weight (e.g. 50–51 kg) and focus on strength and definition there.

## 1. Nutrition (Layne Norton, Rhonda Patrick, Stacy Sims)

### Principles
- **Protein:** 1.6–2.2 g/kg for muscle retention while in a deficit (Norton). To get to 49 kg without losing the “shredded” look, aim **~90–110 g/day** (e.g. 25–35 g per meal + snacks). Non-negotiable when cutting.
- **Energy:** Moderate deficit to lose ~0.25–0.5 kg/week (about 200–400 kcal below maintenance). Too aggressive = muscle loss, low energy, risk to cycle (Sims). You’re very active — eat enough to train hard and recover.
- **PCOS / lipids:** Prioritise whole foods, fibre, healthy fats; your staples (buckwheat, natto, eggs, meat, cheese, rice) fit. Add oily fish or algae omega-3 (Patrick), vegetables, consistent meals for blood sugar and lipids.

### Using your favourite foods
| Food    | Use in plan |
|---------|-------------|
| Buckwheat | Carbs around training, fibre, minerals |
| Natto   | Protein, fibre, K2, gut health |
| Eggs    | Protein, choline, fat-soluble vitamins (breakfast/post-training) |
| Rice    | Easy carbs pre/long run, recovery |
| Meat    | Protein + iron (especially if you run a lot) |
| Cheese  | Protein + fat in moderation; watch if it displaces vegetables/fibre |

### Simple structure (adjust to appetite and training)
- **Meals:** 3–4 solid meals with protein + veggies + carb (buckwheat/rice) + fat.
- **Timing:** Protein spread across the day; carbs around runs and strength sessions.
- **Pace:** **~0.25–0.5 kg per week** toward 49 kg. Slower = better muscle retention and more “shredded” at goal weight.

## 2. Training (Peter Attia, Stacy Sims, Huberman)

### Priority order
1. **Strength (3–4×/week, gym)** — non-negotiable for Stesha-style look and “very strong” (Attia: strength = longevity).  
2. **Running (outside, varied sessions)** — keep the love of long distance but add variety so you’re fit, lean, and not only slow-twitch.  
3. **Yoga** — mobility, recovery, stress.  
4. **Recovery** — sleep, rest days, Whoop; don’t stack too many hard days in a row.

### Strength (gym) — defined abs, slim toned legs & arms
- **Legs:** Squats, lunges, RDLs, step-ups, calf raises — moderate weight, full range, control. Goal: shape and definition like Stesha, not bulk.
- **Arms/shoulders:** Rows, push-ups, overhead press, pull-downs, biceps/triceps — 2–3 sets, 8–15 reps. Builds the lines in arms and shoulders.
- **Core:** Planks, dead bugs, anti-rotation, hollow holds — keeps abs visible as you lean out.

**Sims:** Women respond well to strength; this style won’t make you bulky.  
**Attia:** 3–4 sessions/week supports both physique and longevity when you’re very active.

### Running (outside) — varied sessions
Mix session types so you stay very active and support the look you want (lean, responsive, not only endurance-heavy):

| Session type      | Purpose | Example (adjust to your level) |
|-------------------|--------|---------------------------------|
| **Long easy**     | Base, fat burn, mental | 60–90 min easy, conversational pace |
| **Tempo**         | Lactate threshold, “strong” feel | 20–40 min at comfortably hard (can speak in short sentences) |
| **Intervals**     | Power, metabolism, leg tone | e.g. 6–10 × 400 m or 1 min hard / 1 min easy |
| **Hills**         | Glutes, quads, power | 8–12 × 60–90 s uphill, jog down recovery |
| **Easy/short**    | Recovery, activity without strain | 25–40 min very easy or yoga instead |

**Example week (very active):** 4 runs (e.g. 1 long, 1 tempo or intervals, 1 hills, 1 easy) + 3–4 gym strength + 1–2 yoga. Use Whoop to balance: if recovery is low, swap a run for easy or rest.

## 3. Recovery, sleep, stress (Huberman, Attia)

- **Sleep:** 7–9 h, consistent schedule — non-negotiable for body comp and hormones (PCOS, cycle).
- **Whoop:** Use strain/recovery to decide hard vs easy days; don’t stack heavy strength and long runs on consecutive days when possible.
- **Stress:** Yoga and activity are good; avoid chronic under-eating and overtraining.
