## Calorie check: Is this amount right for your lifestyle? (Evidence-based)

**Your stats:** 31F, 55 kg, 160 cm. **Activity:** 4 runs/week (easy, tempo, intervals, long), 3–4 gym sessions, yoga, 8k–10k+ steps — **very active**.

**Rough numbers (standard equations):**
- **BMR** (Mifflin-St Jeor): ~1,230 kcal (baseline at rest).
- **TDEE** (total daily burn): with your activity level, multiplier is usually **1.55–1.85** (moderate to very active). So **TDEE ≈ 1,900–2,250 kcal/day** depending on the week. Taking a middle estimate: **~2,000 kcal/day** is a reasonable working number.

**Whoop “1,300 kcal”:**
- If Whoop means **total daily burn**, that would be barely above BMR — too low for someone who runs and lifts 6–7×/week. Possible if the device is set to “sedentary” or only counts “active” burn in the main number; worth checking in the app (total vs active vs resting).
- If 1,300 is **active calories only**, then total burn = BMR + 1,300 ≈ 2,530, which is high; possible on big days but not “average.”
- **Conclusion:** For your lifestyle, **assume TDEE ~1,850–2,100** until you have a few weeks of weight trend. Don’t anchor intake to 1,300 unless you’ve confirmed what Whoop is actually reporting.

**What Norton, Sims, and Attia say:**
- **Layne Norton:** In a deficit, keep it **moderate** (e.g. 20–25% below maintenance, or ~400–500 kcal deficit). Too large a deficit increases muscle loss and is hard to sustain. For TDEE 2,000 → intake **~1,500–1,600** for ~0.4–0.5 kg/week.
- **Stacy Sims:** **Active women underfueling** is a major issue — more muscle loss, worse recovery, hormonal impact (relevant for you with PCOS). She stresses: eat enough to train and recover; deficit should not compromise performance or cycle. So: **enough energy on hard days**, lower only on easy/recovery days.
- **Peter Attia:** Sustainability and muscle retention over speed of loss; avoid aggressive cuts when training hard.

**Recommended intake band for you:**
- **Easy / recovery days** (e.g. Mon easy run, Sun yoga): **~1,350–1,450 kcal** (deficit ~500–650 vs 2,000).
- **Quality / long run / gym days** (Tue, Wed, Thu, Fri, Sat): **~1,500–1,650 kcal** (deficit ~350–500). On the hardest days (e.g. long run + gym in same week), **up to ~1,700** is fine so you don’t underfuel (Sims).
- **Weekly average:** **~1,450–1,550 kcal** → expected **~0.3–0.5 kg/week** fat loss with muscle preserved if protein and strength are in place.

**If you were eating 1,050–1,300:** That would be a **700–950 kcal deficit** on a 2,000 TDEE — too aggressive for your volume. Risk: muscle loss, fatigue, worse runs, and possible impact on cycle (Sims). The plan below is revised so daily totals sit in the **1,350–1,650** band (and up to ~1,700 on the biggest day).

## Nutrition principles (evidence-based)

| Principle | Why (voice of nutrition science) |
|-----------|-----------------------------------|
| **Protein 90–100 g/day, spread across meals** | Norton: 0.4–0.55 g per kg per meal for muscle protein synthesis; preserves muscle in a deficit. Sims: critical for active women in calorie deficit. |
| **Carbs around training** | Carbs fuel quality runs and gym; timing around sessions supports performance and recovery without overshooting calories (Norton). |
| **Slightly more on hard days** | Tempo (Tue), intervals (Thu), long run (Sat), gym (Wed/Fri): small bump in carbs/calories so you train well and retain muscle. Easy run (Mon) and recovery (Sun) can sit at the lower end. |
| **Fat from whole foods** | Omega-3 (fatty fish, algae), nuts in small amounts, olive oil — lipids, hormones, satiety (Patrick). Important with PCOS and cholesterol. |
| **Fibre + vegetables** | Fullness, gut health, blood sugar (Patrick). Leafy greens, cruciferous, varied veg daily. |
| **No rigid “good/bad”** | Your favorites (buckwheat, natto, eggs, rice, meat, cheese) stay in the mix; we add foods that support training and body comp (Greek yogurt, salmon, legumes, sweet potato, berries, etc.) per top-tier recommendations. |

## Practical tips

- **Weights:** Cooked grains = after cooking. 80 g cooked ≈ 25–30 g dry buckwheat/rice.
- **Oil:** 1 tsp ≈ 5 g (~45 kcal). Use olive or rapeseed for cooking/dressing.
- **Greek yogurt:** 0–2% fat fits goal; swap for natto or cottage cheese if you prefer.
- **Salmon/fatty fish:** 1–2×/week for omega-3 (Patrick); supports lipids and inflammation.
- **Protein powder:** Optional on heavy days to hit 90+ g without extra volume.
- **Don’t go below ~1,350 kcal** on easy days or cut protein — aggressive deficits undermine muscle and recovery (Sims, Norton). If weight drops too fast (>0.5 kg/week) or energy/cycle suffer, add 100–150 kcal (e.g. extra carb or fat) and hold for 2 weeks.
