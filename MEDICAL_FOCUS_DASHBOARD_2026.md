## Goals vs medical reality (direct)

**49 kg + very lean/shredded + marathons + frequent HIIT** is **possible for some people**, but for **your** profile it is **high risk** if done all at once:

- **Vascular/lipid history:** chronic extreme HIIT + aggressive fat loss can be a poor match for long-term CV risk management unless lipids are optimised and training is periodised.
- **PCOS + unstable cycles:** large deficits + high stress load often worsen cycle regularity and recovery (Stacy Sims–style “energy availability” point).
- **Marathon + peak leanness:** physiologically in tension — marathon blocks favour sustainable fuelling; “shredded” often needs controlled deficit and recovery — **sequence** matters (e.g. marathon block → then short physique focus).

**Honest verdict:** The combo is **not “wrong,”** but it should be **prioritised and periodised**:  
1) lipids + medical follow-up,  
2) stable cycle/energy,  
3) **1–2** hard interval/HIIT days per week (not daily),  
4) marathon training mostly **easy volume**,  
5) physique phase with **moderate** deficit and **high protein**.
